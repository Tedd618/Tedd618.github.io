Parsing 2,300 Course Prerequisites by Hand

soon

Posted on 14th May 2026  |  Categories: Engineering, Parsing

A few weeks ago, I shipped UTM Degree Explorer — a local-first degree planner that validates every prerequisite in your plan in real time. To get there, I had to turn the UTM Academic Calendar's prose into something a computer could reason about.

The calendar describes prerequisites as natural language: "1.0 credit in LIN at the 200-level", "CSC148H5 (or equivalent), excluding CSC209H5", "Any 4.0 credits, including at least one of MAT102H5 or MAT133Y5". A rule-based parser felt fragile. An LLM felt expensive and non-deterministic. So I started with the rules.

A figure would go here.

The trick was treating each prerequisite as an AST — COURSE, AND, OR, CREDITS, LEVEL_POOL, with a RAW fallback for the irreducible cases. After five passes the parser covers ~86% of programs cleanly, and the remaining 14% degrade gracefully into a "see calendar" badge instead of a hard failure.
